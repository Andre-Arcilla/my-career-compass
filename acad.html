STEM
STEM education focuses on science, technology, engineering, and mathematics. Students learn about physics, chemistry, biology, and environmental science, as well as computer science, information technology, and electronics. They also explore engineering principles, design processes, and problem-solving techniques. The STEM strand equips students with skills for careers in STEM-related fields.

    The Humanities and Social Sciences track is a secondary education system that focuses on subjects related to humanities, social sciences, and other fields. It includes subjects like literature, philosophy, history, languages, and arts, as well as sociology, psychology, anthropology, political science, economics, and geography. The track encourages students to develop strong research and analytical skills, effective communication, and cultural awareness. It prepares students for careers in education, journalism, social work, public administration, law, and various social sciences roles.

    The Accountancy, Business and Management (ABM) strand prepares students for careers in financial management, business management, corporate operations, and accounting. It equips students with industry knowledge and skills for successful careers in management, accounting, and business ownership. The strand covers basic principles of business areas like marketing, finance, accounting, information technology, and entrepreneurship, preparing students for crisis management and problem-solving in the workplace.

    The General Academic Strand accommodates students who haven't decided on a specific college course or degree. It is structured to allow undecided learners the flexibility to pursue any college program later on. However, students in this strand may or may not be required to undergo bridging programs (depending on the school) to cover subjects not taken during Senior High.
    Specialized Subjects:
  • Liberal Art and Humanities: English, History, Philosophy & Psychology
  • Social Sciences: Political Science, Anthropology, Economics
